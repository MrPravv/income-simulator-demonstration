Structural Time-Series Mathematical Formulas

Daily Processing Velocity = N_cells × 18 companies/day

N_cells: Number of internal distribution cells active in the market.

18: The fixed empirical limit of un-automated businesses one cell can process per day.

V_ineff = M_size_group × 144 hours × C_h × 0.8

V_ineff: Monthly absolute value of inefficiency (money burned) inside a specific company tier.

M_size_group: Average amount of target personnel in the tier (Small, Medium, Enterprise).

144 hours: The standardized baseline monthly working hours.

C_h: Average cost of an hour of human labor in the selected geographic continent.

0.8 (Locus Coefficient): The permanent "tax on human nature". Even when automated, 20% of biological inefficiency remains.

Dynamic Global Burn Rate = Initial Global Burn Rate - Σ(Active Clients × V_ineff)

Dynamic Burn Rate: Unlike static models, as Antimatter acquires clients, the world physically stops burning this money. The global inefficiency pool decreases linearly by the exact amount of 80% value extracted by active software.

Cross-Sell Compound Multiplier (M_prod) = Σ [from i=1 to N_prods] (K_cross × 0.8)^(i-1)

M_prod: The overall ecosystem value multiplier per client.

K_cross: Cross-sell efficiency percentage (probability of buying the next product).

0.8: Utility decay factor. Each subsequent product solves a slightly smaller routine problem than the previous one.

Daily Revenue = Active Clients × (V_ineff / 30) × M_prod × P_take

Active Clients: Companies that have successfully passed the Free Trial and elasticity checks.

(V_ineff / 30): The daily value generated for the client.

P_take: Your Take Rate (the percentage of saved capital you charge as a SaaS fee).
